Managing AI Solutions in the Energy Sector

  1. Work Experience 2024-Now
Leading AI Strategy and Delivery in a Complex Enterprise Environment

Intro

A year ago a large energy client wanted to dive into AI and leverage the new LLM models that were emerging. I had the opportunity to lead this high-impact AI consulting engagement. As a managing consultant, I was responsible for driving the architecture, strategy, and design of AI applications that touched hundreds of users. I learned a lot!

Building Automated AI Solutions

Our team included three talented developers, one sharp project manager, and a top notch director. We worked with functional partners, corporate teams, security teams, and field users. Together, we designed and deployed a custom Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) application, enabling over 500+ employees and contractors to ask any question about company policies and data. These ChatGPT-like assistants not only boosted productivity but also unlocked siloed knowledge across the organization (with role based access controls). In addition to the RAG app, we developed a suite of AI-powered automations and workflows, including:

  • Image tagging pipelines
  • Vendor receipt processing
  • Agentic, structured numerical analysis for reports

To support innovation across the business, I created a use case intake and prioritization framework. This allowed stakeholders to submit ideas for AI automation, which we tracked and evaluated based on impact, feasibility, and risk. This framework brought transparency, scalability, and alignment to our AI investments. I also created an "engagement model" which enabled any team within the technology organization to onboard to our AI platform and use our AI assistants, knowledge, and guardrails to integrate AI into their workstreams.

Key Learnings

Leading this initiative taught me a great deal—both about building production-grade AI systems and about the organizational dynamics required to make them successful.

1. Strategic AI Governance Matters

I learned how to design an AI governance plan that balanced innovation with oversight. This included defining model lifecycle stages, responsible data usage practices, and alignment with corporate risk policies.

2. Change Management in Emerging Technology

Deploying AI in a risk-averse, regulated environment taught me that change management can’t be an afterthought. I learned to communicate clearly, anticipate objections, and work with legal, security, and compliance teams to pave the way for adoption.

3. Hybrid Infrastructure Is a Reality

We implemented solutions that ran across both on-premises and cloud infrastructure. I gained valuable experience setting up hybrid AI systems—ensuring security and performance while maintaining flexibility for future growth.

4. Agents, Prompts, and Evaluation—Done Right

I developed efficient workflows for prompt engineering, autonomous agent creation, and evaluation. Building useful AI isn't just about models—it's about iterating quickly, measuring well, and staying user-focused.

5. Executive Communication with Tailored Experiences

Throughout the project, I regularly presented to the CIO, CDO, Directors, and the Security team. These conversations sharpened my ability to distill complex technical topics into strategic business insights. I also sent weekly status reports to stakeholders, tracked risk mitigation actions, and maintained a predictable scrum cadence with the team.

Leading Beyond the Project

In parallel to this client work, I also served as the Cohort Lead for our Dallas office, supporting other managing consultants. I coordinated town halls, listened to concerns, and acted as a bridge between leadership and the team. Balancing client delivery with internal leadership taught me how to lead across multiple domains and maintain empathy while driving results.

Final Thoughts

This project deepened my appreciation for the intersection of people, process, and technology. Delivering AI value isn’t just about building models—it’s about aligning stakeholders, managing risk, and empowering teams. I’m proud of what we built, and even more excited about the road ahead.

Streamlining Divestiture: A Technology Consultant's Journey with a Hedge Fund

Introduction

As a technology consultant for a large hedge fund, I had the opportunity to work on a crucial project during a divestiture. In this article, I will share my experiences and highlight how I helped the company go from not having an internal IT organization to the implementation of a powerful SQL server data warehouse and a user-friendly full-stack .NET and Angular web portal. Furthermore, I will discuss the automation of processes for weekly risk reporting, which ultimately provided the company with valuable insights for making informed investment decisions.

Building the Foundation: SQL Server Data Warehouse

During the divestiture, one of the primary challenges was consolidating and organizing vast amounts of financial data. To address this, I spearheaded the creation of a robust SQL server data warehouse. By designing a scalable and optimized database schema, we successfully integrated multiple data sources (internal and 3rd party) and established a centralized repository for all relevant financial information. This allowed the company to access real-time, accurate data for analysis and decision-making.

Enabling Self-Service: Full-Stack .NET and Angular Web Portal

Recognizing the need for streamlined processes, we developed a full-stack web portal using .NET and Angular. This portal empowered accountants within the organization to generate self-service reports and input critical financial information. Each accountant in the organization had a customized landing page showing them issues that needed to be fixed, reports that were ready, and differences between closing timeframes. By using continual feedback loops with these accountants, we ensured that the portal provided a seamless experience for users, enabling them to intuitively access the data they required. This self-service capability significantly reduced dependency on growing IT resources and enhanced overall productivity.

Risk Reporting

A key part of this hedge fund's weekly process was to review the different investing metrics of their accounts (IIR, Mod Dietz, etc.). We constructed reports that visualized these metrics and provided an automated real-time portal for tracking the differences in these metrics week-over-week. These high-visibility reports allowed the business to discuss and take action in an aligned and informed manner. It also allowed accountants to know how to quickly memorialize lines at the end of quarters.

Lessons Learned

Working as a technology consultant during this divestiture provided invaluable lessons that I would like to share:

  1. Collaboration is key: Successful technology initiatives require close collaboration between business stakeholders and IT teams to align objectives, understand requirements, and deliver effective solutions.
  2. Scalability is essential: When building technology solutions for large-scale operations, scalability should be a top priority. Anticipating future growth and designing systems that can handle increasing volumes of data will save time and resources in the long run.
  3. User experience matters: Prioritizing usability and designing intuitive interfaces significantly improve user adoption and overall productivity. Regular user feedback and iterative design processes can ensure that technology solutions meet the needs of end-users effectively.
  4. Automation brings efficiency: Identifying manual processes that can be automated not only saves time but also reduces errors and enhances data accuracy. By automating repetitive tasks, valuable resources can be redirected to more strategic activities.

Conclusion

Some time after the divestiture, my client's IT organization had grown to a point where we could train and transition these platforms to their new internal team. I stayed on the account the last months and trained them to be able to maintain the systems we had put in place. These experiences, as a technology consultant for a large hedge fund during a divestiture, taught me the importance of well-executed IT strategies. By implementing a SQL server data warehouse, a full-stack .NET and Angular web portal, and automating weekly risk reporting, the company gained better insight into its investments and improved operational efficiency. The lessons learned from this project will continue to guide me in future endeavors, as I strive to deliver impactful technology solutions in the dynamic world of finance.
